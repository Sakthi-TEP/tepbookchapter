Submission Guidelines

Chapter Length

5,000 - 8,000 words including references, tables, and figures

Format

Microsoft Word (.doc/.docx), APA 7th Edition

Deadline

December 31, 2025

Eligibility

Book chapter submissions are accepted from undergraduate and postgraduate students, research scholars, faculty members, academicians, and independent researchers.

Authorship

A maximum of four (4) authors is permitted per book chapter. The order of authorship should be finalized before submission.

Scope and Relevance

Chapters must be relevant to the theme of the edited book and should focus on recent trends, original research, reviews, or emerging applications in the respective discipline.

Originality Requirement

Submissions must be original, unpublished, and not under consideration for publication elsewhere.

Plagiarism Policy

All chapters are subjected to plagiarism screening. Manuscripts exceeding acceptable similarity limits will be rejected or returned for revision.

Peer-Review Process

Every chapter undergoes a peer-review process to maintain academic quality, technical accuracy, and relevance.

Formatting and Template

Authors must prepare chapters strictly according to the prescribed template and formatting guidelines provided by the publisher or editorial board.

Language and Style

Chapters should be written in clear academic English, maintaining coherence, logical flow, and proper technical terminology.

References and Citations

Proper citation and referencing must be followed using the recommended reference style specified for the book.

Revision Policy

Authors are required to revise and resubmit chapters based on reviewer and editor comments within the stipulated time.

Submission Mode

All chapters must be submitted in soft copy format through the designated submission system or email as specified.

Publication Charges

Applicable publication charges, if any, will be communicated to authors prior to final submission.

Copyright and Rights

Authors grant the publisher the right to publish and distribute the chapter in print and digital formats, while retaining rights for academic and non-commercial use.

Ethical Responsibility

Authors are responsible for maintaining academic integrity and ethical research practices. Any misconduct may result in rejection or withdrawal.

Final Decision

The decision of the editorial board regarding acceptance, revision, or rejection of a chapter shall be final.

Important Notice

All submissions must be original work not previously published or under consideration elsewhere. Plagiarism checks will be conducted using industry-standard software.
